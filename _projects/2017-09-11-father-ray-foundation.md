Father Ray Foundation began as a plan to teach accessible web development to blind and visually-impaired students at the Father Ray Foundation in Pattaya, Thailand. I designed the curriculum—an introduction to accessible web development grounded in usability heuristics and WAI-ARIA—and worked through the logistics of getting there with collaborators Adrian and Derek.

The heart of the project is Component Blocks: a tactile teaching aid that turns web UI into something you can hold. Students arrange physical blocks representing buttons, inputs, and images on a magnetic whiteboard, label them with markup, and print scannable codes that a companion app reads back aloud like a screen reader. It teaches the structure of a web page through touch and sound rather than sight.
