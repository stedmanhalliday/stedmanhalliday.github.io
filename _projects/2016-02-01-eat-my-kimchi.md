Eat My Kimchi is a card-feed web platform where Korean women anonymously post statements about their experiences with misogyny—the things, as the site puts it, "you could never say out loud." I built the working front-end prototype for CalArts MFA candidate Iris Chung, whose thesis the platform anchored; the bold red-on-white identity and editorial direction are hers, and I developed them into a functional site for her thesis presentation.

The prototype covers the full reader and contributor experience: a statement-card feed, an archive of permalinked posts, and a composer framed with a playful "Nutrition Facts" conceit that rates each statement's spiciness. I built it as a PHP and jQuery site with a LESS-based design system and custom Akkurat and Franklin Gothic typography, translating Chung's mockups into something her audience could actually click through and post to live.
