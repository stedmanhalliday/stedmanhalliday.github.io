[Realistic Manifesto](http://stedmanrh.github.io/realistic-manifesto) reinterprets Naum Gabo and Antoine Pevsner's 1920 Constructivist text as an interactive kinetic sculpture in the browser. The manifesto's five tenets appear as display type arranged in depth while meshes drift and rotate through a 3D rendering of the S. Mark Taper courtyard at CalArts—using direction, depth, and motion to make a viewer aware of space and time, which is exactly what the original text argued sculpture should do.

I built it as a real-time 3D web experience you move through rather than read.
