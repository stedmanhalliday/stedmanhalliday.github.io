Human-Powered Flight is a large-format information poster on the long, strange pursuit of flying under your own power: fixed-wing, flapping, and rotor designs, the Kremer prizes, and the engineering that finally got a person airborne on muscle alone. I designed it as a 30×40-inch foldout that reads from overview down to mechanics and history at detail scale.

It works as a single dense composition you take in by zooming in—a timeline and quotes up top, the aircraft taxonomy and physics below.
