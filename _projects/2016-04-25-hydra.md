The Hydra is a real-time audiovisual instrument I prototyped for performing animation in time with music. A performer loads sliced graphic layers into a composition, uses pads and keys to set their position, scale, and rotation, then times animation events to a tapped tempo—syncing visuals to a beat live, the way a musician would.

I built it across Quartz Composer, ChucK (for Euclidean rhythms and MIDI), Ableton Live, and After Effects, and wrapped it in a bright geometric identity around an initial brief of "water." It's aimed at choreographers, motion designers, and VJs who want rhythmic control over visuals without rigging up a full motion-graphics pipeline.
